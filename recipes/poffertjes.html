Poffertjes

Dutch Poffertjes

If you can`t quite manage a pannenkoeken, then why not have the next best thing poffertjes. Made with yeast and buckwheat flour, these small, fluffy pancakes have a light and spongy texture and are a popular food at Dutch festivals and events.

Food stalls usually serve them warm on a piece of cardboard paper with powdered sugar, butter, or syrup (stroop). They are cooked in special poffertjes pans, which have lots of shallow indentations in them.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups plus 1 tablespoon warm milk
  • 1 cup buckwheat flour
  • 1 cup white all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, more for serving
  • Confectioners' sugar, strawberries, and whipped cream, optional, for serving

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Dissolve the yeast in 1 tablespoon of the warm milk in a small bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the buckwheat flour, white flour, eggs, sugar, salt, half the warm milk, and the yeast and milk mixture. Whisk the ingredients until the mixture is smooth.
  4. Add the remaining warm milk and beat again.
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and allow to rest for an hour.
  6. Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a frying or poffertjes pan.
  7. When the butter starts to sizzle, add teaspoonfuls of the batter in circular movements to create the mini pancakes.
  8. Turn the poffertjes around as soon as the bottom has set, using two forks.
  9. Serve the poffertjes with butter and sieved powdered sugar.
  10. For a sweeter treat, serve these mini pancakes with strawberries, whipped cream, and a dusting of powdered sugar.
